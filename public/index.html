Visit https://liam-swift.itch.io/ to see my games. This is not a complete list of everything I've ever made, but it's a nice selection of "released" things.

I've been making games since I was 10 years old (around 2007). Back then, I used Game Maker 6/7/8/Studio. Since then, I have also dabbled in Unity (mostly), a little bit of Unreal Engine, and Godot.

Creating games was probably the kickstart for my interest in Software Development. Game Maker offered GML (Game Maker Language), which was the first programming language I ever learned.

You can also find plenty of public programming projects of mine on GitHub.

You can find my music on Spotify.

I've been composing since I was 12 years old (around 2009). I use FL Studio for every track, writing in a variety of genres (though mostly electronic).

I've never really educated myself on music theory, rather I just write music with the primary goal of expressing my own emotions and experiences.

When you're an indie dev, you have to pick up a lot of skills. 3D modeling was one of those things I had to learn if I wanted to survive in the wild.

On the right, you will find a showcase of various 3D models I've made, all of them for a neat game in progress by Kyle, Cristina & I called Monica's Paradox.

In july 2025, I joined CGMA Level Design for Games by Shane Canning to learn more about level design. I had always been interested in the different environments in games, particularly the layouts and spaces that the player interacts with.

The ten weeks were insightful, taking me between 10-20 hours each weekend to finish the assignments. Getting personalized feedback from Shane Canning, a professional level designer, was invaluable. I'm grateful to him and CGMA for the experience this gave me.

If you'd like to play the levels I made for this course, you can download it from Google Drive.
